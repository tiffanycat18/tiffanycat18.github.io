Chapter 2: When The World Became Two

I remember having a friend,
persistently trailing my steps,
resisting my efforts to push him away,
providing comfort in moments of darkness,
and despite the tightly closed door,
holding a loyal presence on the red rug outside my bedroom.
I remember how my world fell apart in a flash the day I witnessed him die in a car accident,
his radiant, innocent, and smiling eyes, as usual,
gazing in my direction, about to run into my arms.
I remember how the days that followed weighed upon my soul.

In the end, all I have are memories: moments I still ache for, my traumas, my melancholy, and the happy times I wish I could hold again. They stay with me, like pages I keep rereading.
Maybe that’s what it means to become stories: we live on in the hearts that remember us.
And I hope I’ll be remembered with love and admiration, even as I wrestle with whether I want remembrance or simply fear being forgotten, abandoned.


If you could see your life from start to finish,
would you change things?
